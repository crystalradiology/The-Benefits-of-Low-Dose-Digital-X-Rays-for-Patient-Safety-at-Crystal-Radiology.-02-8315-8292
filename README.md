What Are Low-Dose Digital X-Rays?
A digital X-ray uses advanced sensors instead of traditional film to capture internal images of the body. These sensors convert X-ray beams into digital data, producing sharp, high-resolution images instantly.
The low-dose component refers to the use of optimised technology that reduces the amount of radiation needed to create these images — often by 40–80% compared to conventional X-rays.
At Crystal Radiology, we use state-of-the-art imaging systems that adhere to Australian radiation safety standards, ensuring that patients benefit from both reduced exposure and superior image quality.

Common Uses of Low-Dose Digital X-Rays
Low-dose digital X-rays are versatile and used across various diagnostic needs, including:
•	Chest X-Rays: To detect lung infections, pneumonia, heart conditions, or early signs of lung cancer
•	Bone and Joint Imaging: To assess fractures, arthritis, osteoporosis, and sports injuries
•	Dental and Facial X-Rays: For oral health evaluations and pre-surgical planning
•	Abdominal X-Rays: To identify bowel obstruction, kidney stones, or other abdominal issues
•	Spine Imaging: To evaluate posture issues, scoliosis, or degenerative disc diseases
Whether you’re visiting for a routine check-up or following an injury, Low-Dose Digital X-Rays at Crystal Radiology ensure safe, precise, and comfortable imaging.

Are Low-Dose Digital X-Rays Safe?
Yes — digital X-rays are considered extremely safe. The radiation exposure from a low-dose digital X-ray is comparable to natural background radiation received over just a few days.
At Crystal Radiology, our radiology team ensures that every scan adheres to the “As Low As Reasonably Achievable” (ALARA) principle — meaning only the minimum necessary radiation is used to obtain diagnostic-quality images.
Our facilities are regularly calibrated and accredited to meet national safety regulations, so you can trust that you’re in the best hands.
